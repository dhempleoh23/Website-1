Animals

Explore the Fascinating World of Animals!

Cats and Dogs

Cats have 26 baby teeth and 30 permanent teeth, while Dogs have 28 baby teeth and 42 permanent teeth.

Hummingbirds

Hummingbirds are the only birds that can fly backwards.

Starfish

Starfish do not have a brain.

Polar bear

Polar bear skin is black!

Mosquito

The world's deadliest animal isn’t a shark, bear, or tiger, but something far smaller – the mosquito and only female mosquitoes bite.

Giant Pacific Octopus

The Giant Pacific Octopus has 3 hearts, 9 brains, and blue blood.

English

Fun Facts to Enhance Language Skills!

Alphabet

The word "alphabet" comes from the first two letters of the Greek alphabet: alpha and beta.

I am

The shortest complete sentence in the English language is "I am."

Set

The word "set" has the highest number of definitions in the English language, with over 430 different meanings.

Queue

The word "queue" is the only word in the English language that is still pronounced the same way when the last four letters are removed.

Bookkeeper

"Bookkeeper" and "bookkeeping" are the only words in the English language with three consecutive double letters

Rhythms

"Rhythms" is the longest English word without any vowels.

Mathematics

Numbers, Patterns, and More!

Zero (0)

Zero is the only number that cannot be represented in Roman numerals.

111,111,111

The number 111,111,111 multiplied by itself equals 12,345,678,987,654,321.

Division (÷)

The symbol for division (÷) is called an "obelus."

Equals (=)

The equals sign "=" was invented by Welsh mathematician Robert Recorde in 1557.

Pi (π)

The number pi (π) is an irrational number, meaning it cannot be expressed as a simple fraction and its decimal representation goes on infinitely without repeating.

Algebra

The word "algebra" comes from the Arabic word "al-jabr," meaning "reunion of broken parts."

Science

Discover the Wonders of Science!

Earth's Atmosphere

The Earth's atmosphere is composed of approximately 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, and 1% other gases.

Lightning Bolt

A lightning bolt can reach temperatures of approximately 30,000 kelvins (53,540 degrees Fahrenheit), which is five times hotter than the surface of the sun.

Human Brain

The human brain can generate approximately 23 watts of power when awake.

Jellyfish

The longest recorded lifespan of a jellyfish is over 4,000 years.

Earth's core

The 0 is believed to be composed primarily of iron and nickel.

Human Body

The human body contains approximately 37.2 trillion cells.
